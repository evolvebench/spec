Although there have been numerous definitions of intelligence levels, we still aim to propose a definition of intelligence levels from the evolutionary perspective of embodied intelligence, which we call the Embodied Intelligence Levels (EvolveBench EIL).

|Level|Brief.|Desc.|
|---|---|---|
|EIL 1 | Strong Body Control | Strong Body Control |
|EIL 2|Advanced Environment Modeling|Environmental cognition, A proper understanding of the environment, along with a reasonable ontology design, enables the adoption of correct actions based on environmental understanding, whether rule-based or learnable.|
|EIL 3|High Autonomy|Task cognition, with clear and specific instructions, enables sound decision-making, local planning, and autonomous task execution when task conditions are met.|
|EIL 3.1|Tool Use||
|EIL 4|Continuous Self-Evolution|Self-cognition, with a sound understanding of the relationship between oneself and the environment, enabling appropriate task handling and reasonable planning, as well as strong environmental adaptability and self-learning capabilities.|
|EIL 5|Effective Social Organization|Organizational cognition， socialization, enabling the proper establishment of task ontology-based clusters, assignment of work, and partitioned execution.|
